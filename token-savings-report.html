Reading this honestly

Per-use savings are measured on real artifacts. The per-session total leans on the frequency assumptions above — change them and the total moves. The tokenizer is a proxy, so treat absolute counts as calibrated estimates; the reduction percentages are the robust part. And the biggest real savers — moves 7, 9, and 10 (spend on the spec, consult the oracle, take the low-hanging fruit) — are under-counted here: a wrong build you never make and a user error a picker prevents are costs you can't fully see, so those three rows are conservative estimates, not measurements. On a subscription plan the dollars are really headroom against your usage limits, not cash.
